Understanding the Difference Between an Invoice and a Receipt

Maintaining clear distinctions between accounting documents is essential for accurate ledgering. An invoice functions as a formal request for payment issued by a merchant prior to account settlement. It strictly details project terms, structured remittance guidelines, tax variations, and line-item breakdowns. Conversely, a business receipt serves exclusively as official confirmation of completed asset distribution, acknowledging that all financial liabilities have been fully resolved.
